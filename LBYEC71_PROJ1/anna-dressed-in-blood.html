In a world of ghosts and ghost hunters, Cas Lowood, a teenager with a knack for killing ghosts finds himself in a new town where he meets his match: A dead, slavic girl that haunts a house where people seemed to have disappeared.     Book Synopsis

    Cas Lowood has inherited an unusual vocation: He kills the dead.

    So did his father before him, until his gruesome murder by a ghost he sought to kill. Now, armed with his father's mysterious and deadly athame, Cas travels the country with his kitchen-witch mother and their spirit-sniffing cat. What he finds instead is a girl entangled in curses and rage, a ghost like he's never faced before.     Book Review

    Anna Dressed in Blood is an unexpected, emotional, humorous, amazing read. Kendare Blake makes use of a fictional world of ghosts and ghost killers but brings in a new flavor into the mix. This added touch is brought by the romantic element of Cas lowood and his love interest, a ghost which he was about to kill, Anna.

    The book gets very interesting as the plot progresses as readers are brought into the life of a girl who is already dead. We learn how she died, sympathize with her, and support the bond of this ghost hunter and the ghost that were brought together in unexpected circumstances.

    All other characters in the story such as Carmel and Thomas are good supporting elements of the book as this brings more life into Cas' already gloomy and morbid world of killing ghosts in town after town. Amidst the action, suspense, and drama in this book there is also a very humorous and relatable element with Cas' story. We as readers see how much more complete he is when Anna came into his life and we cherish this very much.

    The ending of the book was bitter-sweet, as Anna disappeared into the abyss with Cas' final moments with her. Readers are left with an empty yet very meaningful ending which brings anticipation for the book's sequel, "Girl of Nightmares."

    Rating:

    4.5/5
